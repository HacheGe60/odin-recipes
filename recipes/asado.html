Asado

Description

Argentine asado is a traditional barbecue centered on slowly grilling beef (and sometimes pork, lamb, and sausages) over wood or charcoal. It’s usually cooked on a parrilla (grill) at low to medium heat so the meat renders and browns gradually, resulting in a smoky flavor and tender texture. Common cuts include ribs (asado de tira), flank (vacío), brisket (tapa de asado), and chorizo and morcilla.

More than just a meal, an asado is a social gathering that can last for hours, where people share food, conversation, and often simple accompaniments like chimichurri, salsa criolla, salads, and bread.

Ingredients

  • Beef cuts (commonly: asado de tira/short ribs, vacío/flank, tapa de asado/brisket cap, matambre/flank steak)
  • Chorizo (pork sausage)
  • Morcilla (blood sausage)
  • Salt (coarse salt is typical)
  • Wood or charcoal (for the fire)

Optional

  • Chimichurri (parsley, garlic, oregano, vinegar/lemon, oil, salt, chili flakes)
  • Salsa criolla (tomato, onion, bell pepper, vinegar, oil, salt)
  • Bread
  • Simple salads (lettuce, tomato, onion)

Steps

  1. Prepare the fire: light wood or charcoal and let it burn down until you have a steady bed of embers (not big flames).
  2. Preheat the grill (parrilla) and clean it. Keep an area with lower heat for slower cooking.
  3. Season the meat: pat it dry and salt it generously with coarse salt (many people salt right before grilling).
  4. Start with the sausages (chorizo and morcilla) and thicker cuts that need more time. Place them on the cooler side first if the heat is strong.
  5. Grill slowly: cook mostly on the bone/fat side first, letting the fat render and the meat cook gently. Avoid moving it too much.
  6. Turn when ready: flip only when the first side is nicely browned and releases easily from the grill.
  7. Manage the heat: add embers as needed and adjust the grill height (or move meat) to prevent burning.
  8. Check doneness: cook to your preference; thicker cuts may take 45–90 minutes or more depending on size and heat.
  9. Rest the meat: remove from the grill and let it rest for 5–10 minutes before slicing.
  10. Serve: slice and serve with chimichurri, salsa criolla, bread, and salads.
