Pascualina

Description

Pascualina is a traditional savory pie from the River Plate region (especially Argentina and Uruguay). It’s typically made with a thin pastry crust and filled with cooked spinach or Swiss chard mixed with sautéed onion, eggs, and ricotta (or another soft cheese). A classic feature is whole eggs placed into the filling so that, when sliced, each portion shows a cross-section of cooked egg. It’s commonly eaten warm or at room temperature and is popular during Lent and Easter, though it’s enjoyed year-round.

Ingredients

  • Puff pastry or pie dough (2 sheets, for top and bottom)
  • Spinach and/or Swiss chard
  • Onion
  • Garlic (optional)
  • Olive oil or butter
  • Ricotta cheese (or another soft cheese)
  • Grated Parmesan (optional)
  • Eggs (some beaten for the filling + whole eggs to place inside)
  • Salt
  • Black Pepper
  • Nutmeg (optional)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 190 °C (375 °F).
  2. Wash the spinach and/or Swiss chard, cook until wilted, then drain very well and chop.
  3. Sauté chopped onion (and garlic, if using) in olive oil or butter until soft and translucent.
  4. Mix the cooked greens with the sautéed onion, ricotta, grated Parmesan (optional), 1–2 beaten eggs, salt, pepper, and a pinch of nutmeg (optional).
  5. Line a pie dish with the bottom sheet of dough, letting the excess hang over the edges.
  6. Pour in the filling and spread it evenly.
  7. Make several small wells in the filling and crack whole eggs into them (usually 3–6, depending on the pie size).
  8. Cover with the top sheet of dough and seal the edges (trim and crimp).
  9. Brush the top with beaten egg, then poke a few small holes or slits for steam to escape.
  10. Bake for 35–45 minutes, or until the crust is puffed and deep golden brown.
  11. Let it rest 10–15 minutes before slicing (or cool to room temperature).
